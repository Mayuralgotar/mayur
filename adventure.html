Satsheri:meaning “seven streams,” is one of the most loved adventure spots near Sihor. Surrounded by lush greenery and rocky hills, this natural waterfall area becomes especially alive during the monsoon. Trekkers and nature lovers often climb through slippery stones and forest paths to witness the scenic beauty and enjoy the peaceful environment. It’s ideal for hiking, photography, and enjoying the sounds of flowing water and birds.

Liliyo Dungar (The Green Hill): Located near the outskirts of Sihor, Liliyo Dungar is a small yet thrilling hillock perfect for trekking and sunrise watching. It offers a moderately adventurous climb and rewards visitors with panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. It’s a favorite among morning walkers, students, and young trekkers looking for a peaceful escape close to nature. During monsoon, the hill turns completely green, adding to its charm.

Gautameshwar Temple and Forest Trail: Situated amidst a natural forest area, Gautameshwar Temple is not only a spiritual destination but also a spot for light trekking and nature walks. The route to the temple involves a scenic drive or bike ride followed by walking trails surrounded by trees, hills, and seasonal streams. The peaceful atmosphere, combined with fresh air and spiritual vibes, makes it a calm yet adventurous escape.

Rataneshwar Mahadev and Cave Trek: This lesser-known spiritual site requires a trek through rocky terrain and forest trails, making it an exciting option for local adventurers. The area includes small caves, Shiva temples, and hidden viewpoints that make the trek enjoyable for both pilgrims and trekkers.

Trekking Around River Shatrunji: River Shatrunji flows near Sihor and creates several small trails and banks that locals use for morning walks and exploring nature. The river area becomes especially scenic during monsoon, with opportunities for group picnics, short treks, and rock balancing activities along its banks.
