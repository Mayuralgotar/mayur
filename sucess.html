Some well known persons...

There are many people from sihor,having important role in history.

Raam Mori: Born on 2 February 1993 in Mota Surka (Sihor), Raam Bhavsangbhai Mori is a celebrated Gujarati short-story writer, screenwriter, and columnist. His critically acclaimed anthology Mahotu (2016) captured rural Saurashtra’s essence, earning him the prestigious Sahitya Akademi Yuva Puraskar in 2017 After completing studies in fabrication engineering, he began writing at age 17, publishing in literary magazines such as Shabdasrishti and Tathapi. Beyond prose, Mori contributes to Gujarati films like Montu Ni Bittu and Mara Pappa Superhero, and writes thought‑provoking columns in Divya Bhaskar and Mumbai Samachar.

Barkat Ali “Befām” Virani: Born 25 November 1923 in Ghanghali near Sihor, Barkat Ali Ghulam Hussain Virani—known by his pen name Befām—was a distinguished Gujarati poet, novelist, and ghazal writer . Starting ghazal writing at age 14, he moved to Mumbai in 1945, later working with All India Radio and contributing lyrics for Gujarati films such as Divadandi (1950). His iconic works Mansar (1960), Ghata (1970), and Pyas (1980) remain cherished in Gujarati literary circles.

Sandip Manubhai Maniar: Born 28 December 1977 in Sihor, Sandip Manubhai Maniar is a former first-class cricketer who represented the Saurashtra cricket team from 2002 to 2013 A right-arm medium-pace bowler and handy lower-order batsman, Maniar competed across formats—First-class, List A, and T20—leaving a strong impression through consistent performance and team spirit.

Keshu Nakarani: Keshubhai Hirjibhai Nakarani served as the Member of Legislative Assembly (MLA) for Sihor from 2007 to 2012, representing the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) First elected in 2007 with 50,756 votes, Nakarani focused on local development and the representation of community interests within the Gujarat Assembly, contributing to infrastructure and constituency welfare .

Nrusinhprasad Kalidas (Nanabhai) Bhatt Born 11 November 1882, in Bhavnagar (then under Bhavnagar State), Nanabhai Bhatt was a visionary educator, freedom fighter, and social reformer whose work had significant impact in Sihor taluka’s rural education. He founded Gram Dakshinamurti in Ambala (in Sihor taluka) in 1938 to promote basic education in villages and served as Minister of Education for Saurashtra State in 1948 Awarded the Padma Shri in 1960, he established Lokbharti Gramvidyapith in Sanosara, which continues Gandhian-inspired rural education.
