1.Roots:Sihor is one of the oldest towns in the Bhavnagar district of Gujarat, with a rich and ancient history. It is believed to date back to the times of the Maitraka dynasty and was once a prominent center of learning and culture.

2.Capital of Gohil Dynasty: Before Bhavnagar city was founded, Sihor was the capital of the Gohil Rajput rulers. Maharaja Bhavsinhji Gohil, the founder of the Bhavnagar State, ruled from Sihor until he moved the capital to Bhavnagar in 1723 for better trade opportunities through the sea.

3.Religious and Cultural Hub: Sihor has been home to many historic temples and stepwells, such as the Brahma Kund, which is over a thousand years old and believed to have spiritual powers. The town reflects the glory of ancient Hindu architecture and Jain influence.

4. Historical Structures: The city still houses old fort walls, gates, and royal palaces, which hint at its regal past. The Sihor Fort and Darbargadh Palace are important landmarks showcasing Rajput architecture.

5. Spiritual Importance: Sihor has long been known for its spiritual energy, attracting sages, poets, and devotees. Saint Vallabhacharya is said to have visited Sihor, adding to its religious legacy.
